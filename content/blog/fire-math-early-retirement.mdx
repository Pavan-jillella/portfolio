# FIRE Math: The Numbers Behind Early Retirement

Financial Independence, Retire Early. The math is simpler than you think.

## The Core Formula

Your FIRE number = Annual Expenses × 25

That's it. If you spend $40,000/year, you need $1,000,000 invested. This is based on the 4% rule — historically, withdrawing 4% of your portfolio annually has been sustainable for 30+ year periods.

```typescript
function calculateFIRENumber(annualExpenses: number): number {
  return annualExpenses * 25;
}

function yearsToFIRE(
  annualIncome: number,
  annualExpenses: number,
  currentSavings: number,
  returnRate: number = 0.07
): number {
  const annualSavings = annualIncome - annualExpenses;
  const target = calculateFIRENumber(annualExpenses);

  let savings = currentSavings;
  let years = 0;

  while (savings < target) {
    savings = savings * (1 + returnRate) + annualSavings;
    years++;
  }

  return years;
}
```

## Savings Rate is Everything

Your savings rate matters more than your income or investment returns.

| Savings Rate | Years to FIRE |
|---|---|
| 10% | 51 years |
| 25% | 32 years |
| 50% | 17 years |
| 75% | 7 years |

The relationship is logarithmic, not linear. Doubling your savings rate more than halves your time to FIRE.

## The 4% Rule Under Scrutiny

The Trinity Study showed that a 4% withdrawal rate survived 95% of all 30-year periods in history. But critics argue:

- Future returns may be lower
- Sequence of returns risk in early years
- 30 years may not be enough for early retirees

My approach: target a 3.5% withdrawal rate for extra safety margin.

## Why the Journey Matters

FIRE isn't really about retirement. It's about:

1. **Freedom** — choosing how you spend your time
2. **Security** — not being dependent on any single income source
3. **Optionality** — the ability to take risks and pursue meaningful work
4. **Skills** — the discipline of high savings rates builds valuable habits

## My Progress

I track three key metrics monthly:

- **Savings rate** — targeting 50%+
- **Net worth** — growing through savings and returns
- **FIRE percentage** — current portfolio / FIRE number

The dashboard keeps me accountable. The math keeps me motivated.
