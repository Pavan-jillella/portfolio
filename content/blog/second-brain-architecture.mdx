# Second Brain Architecture for Engineers

Your brain is for having ideas, not holding them. Here's how I externalize knowledge into a system I can actually rely on.

## The Architecture

My second brain has three layers:

1. **Capture** — Get everything out of your head
2. **Organize** — Structure it for retrieval
3. **Create** — Transform notes into output

## Tools

- **Obsidian** — Daily notes, knowledge graph, permanent notes
- **Notion** — Project management, shared documents
- **GitHub** — Code snippets, documentation, this website
- **Paper notebook** — Morning brain dumps, sketches

## The PARA Method (Modified)

I organize everything into four categories:

- **Projects** — Active work with deadlines
- **Areas** — Ongoing responsibilities (health, finance, career)
- **Resources** — Topics of interest (programming, investing, education)
- **Archive** — Completed or inactive items

## Daily Workflow

1. Morning: Review today's tasks, brain dump new ideas
2. During work: Capture everything in a daily note
3. Evening: Process daily note into permanent notes
4. Weekly: Review and connect notes across topics

## The Power of Links

The magic happens when notes link to each other. Over time, a knowledge graph emerges. Ideas connect across domains. Patterns become visible.

## Why Engineers Should Care

As engineers, our most valuable skill is problem-solving. A well-organized second brain means you're not solving the same problems twice. Every solution, every debugging session, every architectural decision is captured and retrievable.

The compound effect of knowledge management is just as powerful as compound interest.
